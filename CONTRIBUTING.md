# Contributing to AI-Assisted Professional Engineering with .NET

Thank you for your interest in AI-Assisted Professional Engineering with .NET.

This project is an open-source technical book that explores professional software engineering practices in the era of AI-assisted development, with a strong focus on architecture, maintainability, engineering discipline, and long-term sustainability.

---

## Current Project Status

The project is currently in an editorial and architectural stabilization phase.

During this stage, the following areas are still evolving:

- Book structure
- Chapter organization
- Terminology
- Examples and diagrams
- Publishing workflows
- Export pipelines
- Release process

To maintain consistency across chapters and languages, direct content contributions are temporarily limited to project maintainers.

---

## How You Can Contribute Today

Community participation is highly appreciated.

The most valuable contributions at this stage are:

- Technical feedback
- Design discussions
- Architecture discussions
- Real-world experience reports
- AI tooling observations
- Documentation suggestions
- Typo reports
- Terminology consistency observations
- Diagram suggestions
- Feature requests
- Questions and constructive criticism

Please use GitHub Issues or Discussions to share ideas and suggestions.

---

## Pull Requests

At the current stage of the project:

- Pull requests containing content changes may not be accepted.
- Pull requests affecting book structure may be postponed.
- Documentation improvements may be considered on a case-by-case basis.
- Large proposals should begin as an Issue or Discussion before implementation.

Once the book reaches a stable baseline, external contributions will gradually be opened.

---

## Editorial Stability

The book is still undergoing editorial and architectural refinement.

Chapter ordering, terminology, examples, diagrams, and publishing workflows are intentionally allowed to evolve during this phase.

To preserve consistency and maintain a coherent voice throughout the book, direct content modifications are currently restricted to maintainers.

---

## Project Philosophy

The project emphasizes:

- Professional engineering practices
- Long-term maintainability
- Architectural thinking
- Deterministic and reproducible workflows
- AI-assisted development
- Continuous learning
- Incremental publishing
- Documentation-first principles

Maintainability and clarity are generally preferred over short-term convenience.

---

## Development Standards

The repository follows modern engineering practices, including:

- Source-controlled documentation
- Reproducible export processes
- Structured release policies
- Versioned publications
- Long-term maintainability
- Continuous refinement

Additional quality gates and automation workflows will be introduced gradually.

---

## Future Contributor Program

External contributions are planned for a future phase of the project.

Once the architecture, publishing workflow, terminology, and chapter structure become stable, contributor guidelines will be expanded to include:

- Writing standards
- Review process
- Pull request requirements
